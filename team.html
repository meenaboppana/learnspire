Learnspire Team

Take a minute to meet our team!

Meena Boppana

Meena Boppana is a senior at Harvard studying computer science. She is the former president of the Harvard University Math Association and founder of Harvard Gender Inclusivity in Math. Meena also loves to sing and founded the group Harvard Mirch acapella. She feels that soft skills including communication and teamwork has allowed her to be an effective leader in these student groups, and will aid her in her future goal of founding a company.

Neha Dalal

Neha Dalal is a senior at Harvard studying a combination of applied math, economics, and data science. She is very passionate about education and youth empowerment: Neha has taught in 5 countries, researched education with the White House Council of Economic Advisors and Stanford Professor Raj Chetty, and worked with multiple education nonprofits. At Harvard, Neha served as the Editor-in-Chief of the Harvard International Review and was also very involved in the Harvard International Relations Council and the Harvard Institute of Politics. In her free time, she enjoys learning new languages and pretending to be a ninja.

Aticus Peterson

Aticus is a senior at Harvard College studying Applied Mathematics in Government. You may recognize him from the video "Harvard Welcomes You," his freshman year intro Chinese class final project. He subsequently took a two year leave of absense to go on a church mission to Taiwan. Since returning he has spent time working in Beijing in healthcare and consulting. After graduating in May, Aticus will be working at a hedge fund based in Boston. Soft skills have been key to most all of his endeavors and opportunities.

Ying Wang

Ying Wang is a Master student in the Human Development and Psychology program with the Child Advocacy Strand. She graduated from University of Wisconsin with a double major of Elementary Education and Early Childhood, as well as Montessori Teaching Certificate. Ying has had extensive amount of experience working in multicultural settings in both China and the United States. Her current focus is to develop a training curriculum for early childhood teachers and parents to understand the importance the first three years of a child. Ying is also very interested in developing programs that foster bilingual development.

Andrew Larracey

Andrew is a senior at Harvard. He worked as an educational consultant at Grays Associate in summer 2015, focusing on market analysis and determining demand opportunities and industry outlooks. He also completed Marine Corps PLC junior plan of study, an intense leadership training cycle designed to prepare candidates to lead United States Marine. He was a multiple sport captain in high school as well as board member of several academic organizations.
